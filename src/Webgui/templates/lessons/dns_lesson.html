Dns Lookup

What is DNS?

DNS or Domain Name Service is a network protocol that translates IP addresses 15.197.167.90, to human readable strings such as playvalorant.com. While the computer may understand an IP address, humans find this random assortment of numbers difficult to remember. Therefore, DNS allows everyone to access the website they want reliably without having to put in much effort. 

 

What is DNS? ..extended

 

When you hit enter in the search bar, your computer initiates a DNS query to the closest DNS server, translating your human readable input to an IP address. This DNS server will provide another IP address to a closer DNS server and this process repeats until you reach the correct webserver.

 

What is a DNS Lookup?

 

A DNS lookup is an enumeration technique that increases your attack surface by querying the DNS servers of a domain for extra information. Although most enterprise networks are correctly configured with access-lists and firewalls to not allow non-public information to leak, even publicly available DNS information can be valuable in finding extra attack vectors.

 

How can we utilise the enumerated information?

 

Most vulnerabilities come from logic flaws and misconfigurations. By increasing our attack surface, the possibility of finding sensitive information, misconfigured endpoints, or vulnerable functionalities in the target scope increases exponentially as it is in human nature to make mistakes. “Hacking” is all about exploiting these mishaps and oversights. As such, the possibilities of using enumerated information comes down to your creativity, intuition and experience.
