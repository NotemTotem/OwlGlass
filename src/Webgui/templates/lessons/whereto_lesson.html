Where to keep learning

The world of cybersecurity is large and complicated but if you are interested in learning more about the offensive side than OwlGlass has to offer here are some of our recommendations.

 

We recommend that you install Kali Linux, Parrot OS or your Linux Distro of choice either as virtual machines or on a secondary boot drive on your computer (you can still hack on windows or mac OS but many industry leading tools are designed for linux). Install some popular tools such as, burpsuite, nmap, or metasploit and learn how to use them.

 

Some learning platforms we recommend are the Hack The Box academy, Try Hack Me CyberSecurity Training, and the Portswigger Web Security Academy. 

 

Once you have a decent amount of experience and knowledge under your belt you can look into practicing your skills. 

If you are particularly interested in the web app side of hacking you can try web app vulnerability disclosure programs (VDP), or try to earn money with bug bounty programs (BBP). Both of these types of programs can be found on Hackerone and Bugcrowd.

 

If you are more interested in hacking standalone machines with various techniques to gain complete control of a system, you can practice with Hack The Box’s wide range of hacking labs. 

 

The world of hacking is massive and there are so many places to keep learning and endless ways to build a career, or a hobby. Remember to stay curious and learn as much as you can and your skills will grow in no time.
